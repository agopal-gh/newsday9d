Elon Musk pulls out of buying Twitter for $44 billion

Elon Musk has announced that he will be pulling out of the $44 billion deal of purchasing Twitter, as the company failed to produce information on spam bots. For context, on 14th April 2022, the richest man on earth announced that he would be buying the popular social media company Twitter for over $44 billion, in order to become the world’s largest shareholder. Subsequently, on 25th April, the board of directors at Twitter unanimously accepted this offer. However, Musk had a list of terms and conditions in a letter filed with the US Securities and Exchange Commission. One of these terms was for Twitter to gather information on Spambots, as Musk had a goal to improve Twitter by removing these. Upon seeing how Twitter had not fulfilled this term, Musk decided that he did not want to work with a company which did not care enough about the deal. Therefore, he has announced that he will be leaving the deal. Unfortunately for Elon though, the price of not completing the transaction is $1 billion. Nevertheless, the Twitter Chairman Bret Taylor tweeted about how the company was going to ensure that the deal was secured. “The Twitter Board is committed to closing the transaction on the price and terms agreed upon with Mr. Musk and plans to pursue legal action to enforce the merger agreement. We are confident we will prevail in the Delaware Court of Chancery.” However, Tesla CEO’s lawyers responded, saying how in the filing Twitter had failed or refused to comply with numerous requests on information about fake or spam accounts. Musk has repeatedly said that the information on these spam bots would prove crucial to Twitter’s business performance, and he had previously openly said how he would cancel the deal if Twitter does not give him this data. "Twitter is in material breach of multiple provisions of that Agreement, appears to have made false and misleading representations upon which Mr. Musk relied when entering into the Merger Agreement," as read the letter. Due to this deal being a failure, many in the general public will feel more relieved such as leftists like those in the Labour Party and other defenders of social media censorship, many of whom despise the prospect of a “free speech absolutist” who reads Breitbart News articles taking ownership of Twitter. Nevertheless, Musk likely will not be able to walk away from the deal, as he supposedly intended, as because of the Twitter board’s obstinance, it may not be entirely over, as they still did not accept the deal and could still sue Musk to complete the takeover.
